Project 1

Chui Shui Unlimited Company

For developers

[CHUI SHUI] Unlimited Company is a company that hires developers to chat every day. 'Chui shui' is a Chinese slang term that means to engage in casual and aimless conversation, and literally translates to 'blowing water' in English. This project aims to improve the relationship between work-at-home (WFH) employees and the company. The percentage of remote workers has increased sharply during Covid-19 lockdown, which has made WFH employees' psychological problems more apparent. WFH workers can easily become disconnected from the organization due to physical isolation. They cannot talk face-to-face, and important cues such as eye contact, tone of voice, body language, and physical touch are easily lost during communication. This physical isolation can lead to social isolation, causing people to feel lonely and depressed during the isolated working process, and behave like cold machines working only for money. This discounts the role of a company as an emotional support organization for an employee. The project aims to rebuild the relationship between WFH employees and their companies.

Inspiration

On February 2020, I began my first remote job as a developer in a US company. As lockdowns spread in China, more people began to join the company. However, I quickly realized that I lacked connections with the entire company. Compared to my previous offline work at the office, frequent communications were limited to a small group of only 3-5 people. I had no contacts with any new colleagues and was not familiar with most of them. I felt isolated while working at home and wondered how to relieve this feeling. Therefore, I decided to study social contacts in a remote company.
